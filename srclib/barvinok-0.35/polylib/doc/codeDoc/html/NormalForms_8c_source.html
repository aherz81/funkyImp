00650 ***   Input  : A Matrix A (The Matrix A is not necessarily a Polylib matrix.
00651 ***             It is just a matrix as far as Hermite is concerned. It

00653 ***   Output : The Hnf matrix H and the Unimodular matrix U such that
00654 ***               A = H * U.
00655 ***
00656 ***   We use Alan Darte's implementation of Hermite to compute the HNF.
00657 ***   Alan Darte's implementation computes the Upper Triangular HNF.
00658 ***   So We work on the fact that if A = H * U then 
00659 ***       A (transpose) = U(transpose) * H (transpose)
00660 ***       There are a set of interface functions written in Interface.c
00661 ***        which convert a matrix from Polylib representationt to that of
00662 ***        Alan Darte's and vice versa.
00663 ***     
00664 ***   This Function Does the Following
00665 ***    Step 1 : Given the matrix A it finds its Transpose.
00666 ***    Step 2 : Finds the HNF (Right Form) using Alan Darte's Algorithm.
00667 ***    Step 3 : The H1 and U1 obtained in Step2 are both Transposed to get
00668 ***              the actual H and U such that A = HU.
00669 **/

00680   darte_id_inv = (Value *) malloc(sizeof(Value) * A->NbColumns*A->NbColumns);
00681   for (i=0; i< A->NbColumns * A->NbColumns; i++)

00688   hermite(darte_matA, darte_identite, darte_id_inv,A->NbColumns,A->NbRows, 1);
